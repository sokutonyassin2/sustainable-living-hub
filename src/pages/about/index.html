About BOS

From a bare five-acre plot to a thriving living laboratory where indigenous knowledge meets agricultural science.

In 2007, I stood on a piece of land in Senga village, in Luweero District of central Uganda.

The five-acre plot was bare. Only one old mutuba tree stood tall, and the soil looked exhausted from years of overuse.

I remember asking myself a difficult question: Can this really work for me?

The land was about 50 kilometres from my home. I had no car, and I couldn't afford one. The road was rough murram, riddled with potholes and so narrow in places that even a small car would struggle to pass.

Still, my desire for farmland outweighed my doubt.

So I paid the money.

Today, a Different Story

Today, that same land tells a very different story.

Where there was once bare ground, there is now a mini forest, thriving crops, insects, birds, and animals. The soil is alive again.

What began as a risky decision has slowly become a living laboratory where indigenous knowledge meets agricultural science, and where ideas about environmental restoration, healthy food, and sustainable farming are tested and shared.

Who I Am

My name is William Odinga Balikuddembe known as the Bill of Senga, or simply BOS.

I am a science journalist, communicator, and farmer.

For more than twenty years, I have reported on agriculture, water, the environment, and rural livelihoods across Uganda and beyond.

Alongside this work, I practice farming transforming a small piece of land into a living space where knowledge, ideas, and innovation grow alongside trees, crops, birds, animals, insects and other things.

Beyond journalism and farming, I serve as Secretary for Research and Head of the Department of Policy and Research at the National Unity Platform (NUP), a party I helped found.

I have held the first role since 2020 and the second since 2023 part of a long-standing commitment to public service, ensuring that research and policy translate into real benefits for communities.
